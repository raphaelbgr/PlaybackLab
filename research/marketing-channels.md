# Marketing Channels for PlaybackLab

A comprehensive list of communities and forums where video streaming developers congregate. These are ideal places to market a Chrome DevTools extension for HLS/DASH debugging.

---

## Reddit Communities

### r/videoengineering
- **Link:** https://www.reddit.com/r/videoengineering/
- **Size:** ~25,000+ members (grew 300% during the pandemic)
- **Activity Level:** High - active discussions daily
- **Focus:** Video production engineering, live streaming, multi-camera setups, codecs, remote production
- **Self-Promotion Rules:** No stupid questions policy, welcoming community. Check sidebar for specific rules. Generally follows Reddit's 90/10 rule.
- **Best Approach:**
  - Become an active member first by answering HLS/DASH technical questions
  - Share debugging tips and troubleshooting advice
  - After building reputation, share your tool when someone has a problem it solves
  - Has a Discord server for more casual discussions

### r/ffmpeg
- **Link:** https://www.reddit.com/r/ffmpeg/
- **Size:** Medium-large, highly technical audience
- **Activity Level:** Moderate - focused on FFmpeg-specific questions
- **Focus:** FFmpeg commands, encoding, transcoding, video/audio processing
- **Self-Promotion Rules:** Standard Reddit 90/10 rule
- **Best Approach:**
  - Help people troubleshoot encoding issues
  - Share HLS/DASH manifest debugging insights
  - Position PlaybackLab as complementary to FFmpeg workflows

### r/webdev
- **Link:** https://www.reddit.com/r/webdev/
- **Size:** Very large (millions of members)
- **Activity Level:** Very high
- **Focus:** Web development broadly, including video integration
- **Self-Promotion Rules:** Strict 9:1 rule - 9 helpful interactions per 1 promotional post
- **Best Approach:**
  - Answer questions about video.js, hls.js, or streaming integration
  - Post in "Show Off Saturday" threads if available
  - Create educational content about debugging video streams

### r/streaming
- **Link:** https://www.reddit.com/r/streaming/
- **Size:** Large
- **Activity Level:** High
- **Focus:** General streaming topics, platform-agnostic
- **Self-Promotion Rules:** Check sidebar rules
- **Best Approach:** Focus on technical streaming questions rather than content creation topics

## General Self-Promotion Tips for Reddit

1. **Follow the 90/10 Rule:** 90% valuable contributions, 10% self-promotion
2. **Be a Community Member First:** Spend 2-4 weeks helping others before promoting
3. **Solve Real Problems:** Only mention your tool when it genuinely solves someone's stated problem
4. **Be Transparent:** Disclose that you're the creator
5. **Add Value:** Every mention should come with helpful context
6. **Don't Spam:** One organic mention per community, then engage naturally
7. **Use Weekly Threads:** Many subreddits have "Self-Promotion Saturday" or similar
